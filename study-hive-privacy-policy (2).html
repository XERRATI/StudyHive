Study Hive Privacy Policy

1. Overview

Study Hive is designed to be local-first and student-friendly. In the current version, most study data is stored in the user’s own browser using localStorage. The creator does not automatically receive notes, flashcards, tasks, subjects, streaks, pledge text, garden data, or study progress unless the user chooses to send information through feedback, email, third-party AI, weather/location, notifications, hosting, or future cloud features.

2. Contact

For privacy questions, data concerns, permission requests, bugs, feedback, or general communication, contact omphemetse.mogale0409@gmail.com.

3. Information Users Enter

Users may enter names or nicknames, goals, goal dates, pledges, subjects, study minutes, tasks, habits, notes, flashcards, formulas, vocabulary progress, exam dates, grades, moods, water tracking, reflections, garden journal entries, feedback messages, email addresses, and Hive Coach topics.

4. Information Generated by the App

The app may generate streaks, XP, achievements, heatmaps, hive progress, bee models, garden flowers, study plans, adaptive quiz prompts, grade predictions, weather status, reminders, review schedules, and other calculated progress data.

5. Local Storage

Most data is saved in the browser. This means it may be lost if browser data is cleared and may be visible to someone with access to the same browser profile. Users should not store highly sensitive information in the app. Export/backup tools should be used for important study data.

6. Feedback and Email

If a user sends feedback or chooses to contact the creator, the message, optional email address, category, rating, and optional non-sensitive context may be sent to omphemetse.mogale0409@gmail.com or through the user’s email app/form service. Feedback is used to respond, debug, improve features, and understand what users need.

7. Privacy Mode

Privacy Mode is intended to reduce data exposure by hiding weather, disabling notification preference, and turning off optional feedback context by default. It does not encrypt local browser storage or prevent a device owner from viewing data.

8. Weather and Location

Weather features may use a manually typed city or browser geolocation permission. If geolocation is allowed, coordinates may be used with a weather provider. Users can deny location permission and still use the app.

9. AI Features

AI-assisted flashcard or question generation may send pasted notes to a third-party AI service if enabled and hosted correctly. Users should not submit private, confidential, or restricted school information to AI tools unless they have permission.

10. Music and Audio

Study Hive may include user-provided music embedded into the app, generated background music, UI sounds, and study ambience. Audio playback happens in the browser. The app does not need microphone access for these sounds.

11. Notifications

If enabled, browser notifications may show milestone or weather-change messages. Users can deny or revoke notification permission in the browser. Notifications may appear on shared devices, so users should enable them carefully.

12. Children and Students

Study Hive is intended for students and should avoid collecting unnecessary personal data. Younger users should use it with guidance from a parent, guardian, teacher, or school where required by law. Schools should review local student privacy rules before deploying it.

13. Third-Party Providers

The app may use services such as Google Fonts, weather APIs, Google Drive-hosted audio, browser APIs, email clients, AI providers, form endpoints, or hosting platforms. Those services have their own privacy policies and may process technical data such as IP address or user agent.

14. Cookies and Tracking

The current concept does not require advertising cookies, analytics cookies, or behavioural ad tracking. Hosting platforms or future integrations could introduce cookies, in which case this policy should be updated and any required consent tools added.

15. Data Sharing

The creator does not sell personal study data. Information may be shared only when the user chooses to send it, when required by law, when necessary to protect rights/safety, when using third-party services, or if the project is transferred to a new operator with appropriate notice.

16. Data Retention

Local data remains until the user deletes it, resets the app, clears browser storage, changes browsers/devices, or storage is otherwise removed. Feedback emails or form submissions may be retained as long as needed to respond, improve the app, or keep records.

17. Security

No app is perfectly secure. Users should protect their devices, avoid entering highly sensitive information, and understand that localStorage is not the same as encrypted secure storage. Hosted versions should use HTTPS and reputable providers.

18. User Choices

Users can avoid optional fields, deny location, disable notifications, turn on Privacy Mode, avoid AI features, clear localStorage, export backups, delete notes/tasks/cards, or stop using the app.

19. Access, Correction, and Deletion

For local data, users can edit or delete data directly in the app or browser storage. For feedback sent to the creator, users can contact the email address above to request deletion or correction where legally required and technically possible.

20. International Transfers

If third-party services are used, information may be processed in other countries. Schools and organisations should evaluate legal transfer requirements before enabling AI, analytics, forms, hosting, or cloud features.

21. Changes to this Policy

This policy may be updated as Study Hive changes. If new accounts, cloud sync, payments, analytics, advertising, or teacher dashboards are added, the policy should be reviewed and updated before release.

22. Ownership and Protection Note

The Privacy Policy does not grant anyone permission to copy Study Hive. Ownership, copyright, copying restrictions, and permission requirements are handled in the Terms of Service.
